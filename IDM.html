In collaboration with my team, we undertook the redesign of the logo for the Master's program in Trinity College Dublin - Interactive Digital Media. After a quick brainstorming session on different representations of the course and a survey on what the course means to other students, we decided that the IDM logo should represent themes of technology/coding. The final design is simple yet informative.

Story

Naming:IDM VS Interactive digital media. Through the survey’s findings, we concluded that an alteration of the course name is unnecessary. If a name alteration were undertaken, it would increase the risk of misrepresenting the course, misaligning it with the target audience’s thoughts and ideas regarding the course and its content.

Concept:We experimented with various concepts, colours and typefaces. After weeks of experimentation, we chose a concept based upon coding languages - such as HTML and Javascript. This concept successfully conveys the technological theme to audiences and looks visually interesting in doing so.

Typography:The group experimented with monospaced fonts and we ultimately chose a monospaced font called ‘Share Tech Mono’. Monospaced fonts convey precision, order and technology. These ideas accurately represent a significant portion of the course, as these qualities are associated with computing and programming.

Color Palette: We initially started with a solid green colour palette but abandoned this idea after concluding that it was too obvious. We experimented with various blues and eventually settled on the hex code #02B2E0.
